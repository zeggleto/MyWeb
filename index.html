Let's see....... I've been working as an associate software developer for Infosys for almost a year now, providing analysis and writing code for custom plugins, scripts, and queries for the Dynamics CRM systems and databases of our client American Water. I've been trained to work with front-end and back-end web development, database administration and querying, as well as managing automated server jobs with Scribe Integration. My fascination for puzzles allows me to enjoy my job, whether it be trying to fix a software bug or finding an efficient way to perform certain tasks.

Back at home, I enjoy video games, watching football or pro wrestling, playing guitar, jigsaw puzzles (yep), and working on some coding projects of my own. I also love the outdoors as well, going hiking whenever I can (sometimes with my ADHD riddled dog) and I'm always up for exploring new places.

I also like maps. No, seriously. I could stare at a map for hours and not get bored. It's a weird obsession of mine that I'm trying to tame into my profession as well. More specifically, why haven't we spread out across more remote areas of the world yet like northern Alaska or central Austrailia? It's starting to get crowded here!

So that's my long term goal; to be able to develop technology that allows us to inhabit places that we right now consider remote or deserted. I love going to places that not many people have been to, and I believe we can find a way to be able to live among these areas one day, and I would very much like to help out with that!
